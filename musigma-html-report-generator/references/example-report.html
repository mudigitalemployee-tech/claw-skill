Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive analysis of 350 e-commerce customers across 6 U.S. cities, examining purchasing behavior, membership engagement, satisfaction drivers, and discount effectiveness. The analysis reveals a highly stratified customer base where membership tier is the single strongest predictor of spend, engagement, and satisfaction.

Key Metrics: Total Customers: 350 | Avg. Total Spend: $845 | Satisfied Customers: 35.7% | Spend–Rating Correlation: 0.94

Critical Finding: 100% of Gold members are Satisfied, yet 0% of Bronze members report satisfaction — a complete satisfaction cliff between tiers. Additionally, customers who received discounts show higher churn risk (66% Unsatisfied) despite the incentive, suggesting discounts are reactive rather than preventive.

Key Insights at a Glance:

  • Membership drives everything: Gold members spend 2.8× more than Bronze ($1,311 vs $473) and are exclusively Satisfied.
  • Discount paradox: Discounted customers spend less ($787 vs $904) and are more dissatisfied (66% Unsatisfied vs 0%).
  • Geographic concentration: San Francisco and New York dominate revenue with 3× higher average spend than Houston/Chicago.
  • Gender gap: Male customers spend 40% more on average ($987 vs $704), driven by higher Gold membership representation.
  • Strong spend–satisfaction loop: Satisfied customers spend $1,280 on average — 2.15× more than Unsatisfied ($595).

Satisfaction Breakdown by Tier

The satisfaction distribution reveals a stark polarization:

  • Gold: 100% Satisfied — every single Gold member reports satisfaction. This tier functions as the "loyal core."
  • Silver: A contested middle ground — 44% Neutral, 50% Unsatisfied, and only 7% Satisfied. These are the customers most at risk of churning or upgrading.
  • Bronze: 50% Unsatisfied, 48% Neutral, 0% Satisfied. No Bronze member reports being satisfied — a red flag for the bottom tier.

Spending Behavior Analysis

Customer spending follows a clear trimodal distribution driven primarily by membership tier, with secondary effects from geography and gender.

Spend Distribution

The spend histogram reveals three distinct clusters corresponding to the three membership tiers — with virtually no overlap between Gold ($1,120–$1,520) and Bronze ($411–$530). Silver occupies the $660–$831 band.

Spend vs. Rating Correlation

The Pearson correlation between Total Spend and Average Rating is r = 0.94 — an exceptionally strong positive linear relationship. Higher-spending customers consistently rate their experience better. This could reflect either a genuine experience advantage for high-spend customers, or a self-selection effect where satisfied customers naturally spend more.

Discount vs. Satisfaction

The Discount Paradox: Customers who received discounts are 66% Unsatisfied compared to 0% Unsatisfied among non-discount customers. They also haven't purchased in 37 days on average vs. 16 days for non-discount customers. This strongly suggests discounts are being applied reactively to already-disengaged customers (Bronze and Silver tiers with low recency), rather than proactively to drive loyalty. The discount itself is not causing dissatisfaction — it's being targeted at customers who are already dissatisfied.

Recency & Churn Risk

Recency is a strong engagement indicator. Unsatisfied customers average 42.8 days since last purchase — 2.4× the Satisfied average (17.5 days). This is primarily concentrated in Silver and Bronze tiers with discounts applied, forming a clear churn-risk segment:

  • High Churn Risk: Silver/Bronze + Discount Applied + Days > 30 → 116 customers (33% of base)
  • Loyal Core: Gold + No Discount + Days < 20 → 117 customers (33% of base)
  • Swing Segment: Silver + No Discount + Neutral satisfaction → key upgrade targets

Conclusion

This e-commerce customer base is sharply segmented by membership tier, with tier functioning as a near-perfect proxy for spend, satisfaction, engagement, and retention. The data tells a clear story:

  • Gold members are the engine — high spend, high satisfaction, high frequency. Protect this segment at all costs.
  • Silver members are the opportunity — they're the swing segment where targeted investment can yield the highest marginal returns.
  • Bronze members need intervention — zero satisfaction is a systemic failure that discounts alone cannot fix.
  • The discount program needs a fundamental rethink — it's currently a symptom tracker, not a solution.

The 0.94 correlation between spend and rating, combined with the perfect satisfaction stratification by tier, suggests that experience quality scales with engagement level. The priority should be moving customers up the tier ladder through better experiences — not throwing discounts at already-disengaged customers.

Next Step: Conduct a controlled A/B test on the Silver tier — test a proactive upgrade incentive program (benefits preview + spend milestone tracker) against the current reactive discount approach. Measure conversion to Gold, satisfaction shift, and 90-day retention.
