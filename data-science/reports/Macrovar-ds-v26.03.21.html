Introduction

This report presents the results of a comprehensive regression analysis for the Macrovar project, conducted using the Mu Sigma modular Data Science pipeline. The analysis covers 10 target variables simultaneously, applying state-of-the-art machine learning and statistical models to generate actionable forecasts.

Target variables analysed:

  1. Coincident Index
  2. X10 Year Treasury Note Yield
  3. SnP500 ETF
  4. Unemployment Rate
  5. Yield Curve
  6. CPI U All Items Less Food Energy
  7. Copper ETF
  8. Spot Oil Price West Texas Intermediate
  9. Risk Premium
  10. US Dollar Index

The analysis follows the Mu Sigma 3-phase modular pipeline, ensuring reproducibility and rigorous model validation at each step: Phase 1 — EDA & Preprocessing (data profiling, feature engineering, outlier detection, train/test split), followed by Phase 2 — Model Selection & Validation (multi-model training, cross-validation, hyperparameter fine-tuning), and finally Phase 3 — Execution & Insights (final inference on the held-out test set, drift analysis, and business insight generation). The outputs of all three phases are assembled into this self-contained interactive report.

Model Training & Selection

Each target variable was modelled independently using 4 candidate algorithms. All models were trained on the same training set with identical cross-validation folds, ensuring a fair head-to-head comparison. The best model for each target was selected based on the primary metric (RMSE for regression), then fine-tuned via hyperparameter search. Overall, RandomForestRegressor emerged as the dominant performer, winning 6 of 10 targets.
