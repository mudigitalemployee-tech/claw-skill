Introduction

This report presents the results of a comprehensive forecasting analysis for the Macrovar project, conducted using the Mu Sigma modular Data Science pipeline. The analysis covers 10 target variables simultaneously, applying machine learning and statistical models to generate actionable forecasts.

Target variables analysed:

  1. Coincident Index
  2. X10 Year Treasury Note Yield
  3. SnP500 ETF
  4. Unemployment Rate
  5. Yield Curve
  6. CPI U All Items Less Food Energy
  7. Copper ETF
  8. Spot Oil Price West Texas Intermediate
  9. Risk Premium
  10. US Dollar Index

Methodology

The analysis follows the Mu Sigma 3-phase modular pipeline: Phase 1 - EDA & Preprocessing (data profiling, feature engineering, outlier detection, train/test split), followed by Phase 2 - Model Selection & Validation (multi-model training, cross-validation, hyperparameter tuning), and Phase 3 - Execution & Insights (final inference on held-out test set, drift analysis, business insight generation).

Appendix: Formulas

Metric Definitions

MetricFormulaInterpretation
RMSEsqrt( (1/n) * sum( (y_i - y_hat_i)^2 ) )Root Mean Squared Error - penalizes large errors more heavily. Lower is better.
MAE(1/n) * sum( |y_i - y_hat_i| )Mean Absolute Error - average magnitude of errors. Lower is better.
MAPE(100/n) * sum( |y_i - y_hat_i| / |y_i| )Mean Absolute Percentage Error - scale-independent accuracy measure. Lower is better.
R21 - sum( (y_i - y_hat_i)^2 ) / sum( (y_i - y_bar)^2 )Coefficient of Determination - proportion of variance explained. Higher is better (max 1.0).
CV Mean(1/k) * sum( score_j ) for j=1..k foldsMean cross-validation score across k folds. Indicates average generalization performance.
CV Stdstd( score_1, ..., score_k )Standard deviation of CV scores. Lower indicates more stable model performance across folds.
